{{< accordion "What is the Let’s Raise Air-wareness campaign? " >}}

Let’s Raise Air-wareness is a community health campaign run by Air is Kin, an environmental health justice project developed by Centric Lab. It aims to connect Hounslow families with information and tools to understand air quality and its impact on health, using trusted school channels to reach the community. 

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Why is this campaign focused on Hounslow?" >}}

Hounslow is one of the areas in London most significantly affected by air pollution. The campaign is hyperlocal by design, ensuring that the families who most need this information are the ones being reached. The ambition is to roll the campaign out to other heavily affected communities over time. 

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Do I need to know a lot about air pollution to teach this lesson?" >}}
Not at all. The Become an Air Detective worksheet and accompanying lesson guide are designed so that any teacher can deliver the activity with confidence, regardless of prior knowledge of the topic. The focus is on curiosity and observation, not scientific expertise. 

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion " Is there a specific curriculum link?" >}}
The activity sits naturally within science (working scientifically, living things and their habitats), PSHE (health and wellbeing, community) and Geography (human and physical geography, fieldwork). It is suitable for both KS1 and KS2 and can be adapted to suit your class. 

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "What if a child becomes anxious about air pollution during the lesson?" >}}
The lesson is designed to be light-hearted and empowering rather than alarming. If a child raises concerns, acknowledge their feelings calmly and redirect to the positive action the campaign encourages - noticing, exploring and talking to trusted adults. Avoid dwelling on health risks in class. 

{{< /accordion >}}
{{< accordion "Can I adapt the worksheet or lesson for my class?" >}}
Yes. The worksheet and lesson guide are flexible resources. You are encouraged to adapt the discussion questions, timing and activities to suit the age, ability and needs of your pupils. If you make adaptations you find particularly effective, the campaign team would love to hear about them. 

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "What should I do if a parent wants more information?" >}}
Direct parents and carers to the Let’s Raise Air-wareness website, where they can find full campaign information, further resources and links to the Air is Kin project: shweatanhegde.github.io/air-is-kin-v2/. A parent and carer guide is also available to send home alongside the worksheet. 

{{< /accordion >}}
{{< accordion "How does this benefit families beyond the classroom? " >}}
The worksheet is intentionally designed as a conversation starter. By bringing it home, pupils create an opportunity for families to talk about their local environment together. The campaign’s wider goal is to encourage parents and carers to seek GP support if they recognise signs that air quality may be affecting their family’s health.

{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Is this a one-off activity or part of a wider programme?" >}}
Become an Air Detective is one resource within the broader Let’s Raise Air-wareness campaign. Additional classroom activities, take-home materials and community resources are available via the campaign website. The campaign is actively developing new resources, so it is worth checking back regularly. 

{{< /accordion >}}
{{< accordion "Who can I contact if I have further questions? " >}}
For any questions about the campaign, resources or how to get more involved, please visit the Let’s Raise Air-wareness website. Information about contacting the Air is Kin team directly can be found there.
