---
title: More Digital Resources by Air is Kin
---
Air is Kin has several digital resources that might come useful at various stages of your journey in understanding the cause and impact of air pollution to your and your community's health. Some key resources are listed below: 

## Health Diary
https://www.thecentriclab.com/air-is-kin/health-diary-info-guide

The Health Diary (including the Symptoms Weekly Tracker and Info Guide) is a self-reporting tool designed for community members to capture and document their lived experiences with air pollution exposure. It features structured, peer-reviewed questionnaires that help individuals track changes in both their environment and health - such as persistent coughs, migraines, and skin reactions. By recording these symptoms, communities can better understand how air pollution affects them and clearly articulate these patterns to healthcare professionals.

## Air is Kin Academy
https://www.thecentriclab.com/air-is-kin/air-is-kin-academy

The AiK Academy is a self-directed, digital learning tool built within an interactive "Miro World." It features a series of modules and lessons covering the history of environmental movements, the science of air pollution, and the specific ways pollutants harm our bodies and minds. The academy is structured to help build an educated, connected community that can move health justice advocacy forward at a supportive and gentle pace.

## Talk to Your GP
https://www.thecentriclab.com/air-is-kin/approach-gp-ap-childhealth

The project addresses how communities interact with primary care through resources like the "Approaching Your GP On Air Pollution-Related Health Concerns" booklets (available for both adults and children). Because current healthcare systems often fail to record air pollution's health impacts in real time, these guides equip people to use their GPs as an institutional "witness." They provide strategies for presenting health diaries and symptom patterns so doctors can formally recognize and connect everyday illnesses to local environmental pollution.

## Sacrifice Zone
https://london.sciencegallery.com/vs-artworks/air-is-kin

The Air is Kin framework defines an urban "Sacrifice Zone" as a geographic area or neighbourhood where pollution has been deemed legally or politically acceptable by structural planning and commercial policy. In these zones, a piece of land and its community are essentially ‘sacrificed’ under the false narrative that the pollution is a necessary trade-off for the greater economic good. To combat the legal loopholes of standard definitions, the project uses geospatial and physiological data to re-define these zones at the point of ‘first harm’, allowing communities to hold industrial polluters accountable early on.
